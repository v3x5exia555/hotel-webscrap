# Pahang Hotel Intelligence Presentation Points

This document outlines how the system addresses key business questions regarding hotel check-ins, occupancy, and market trends. Use these points for your presentation.

---

## 1. Tracking Check-ins & Popularity

**Question:** _"Which hotels are getting the most check-ins?"_

### Talking Points:

- **The "Pickup" Method**: We don't have access to the hotel's internal reception desk, so we use **Difference Tracking**. By comparing inventory at Time A and Time B, we calculate the "Pickup" (rooms sold).
- **Top Performers**: The dashboard identifies hotels where inventory drops fastest. This indicates high demand and frequent check-ins.
- **Revenue Mapping**: We multiply the "Pickup" count by the room price captured at that moment to estimate the daily revenue being generated by each property.

---

## 2. Granular Occupancy Monitoring

**Question:** _"For a specific hotel on a specific date, which rooms had no check-ins?"_

### Talking Points:

- **Inventory Stagnation**: If the "Rooms Left" count for a specific property remains unchanged over 24-48 hours, it is a high-confidence indicator that no bookings (check-ins) occurred for that period.
- **Supply vs. Reality**: We compare the **OTA Supply** (what they offer online) against their **Official Declarations**. If a hotel shows "10 rooms left" but declares "0 occupancy" to the state, the system flags this as a potential reporting discrepancy.
- **Wait-and-See Data**: While we don't see specific room numbers (e.g., Room 101), we see the **Product Category** (e.g., "Deluxe King Room"). We can tell you if a specific category had zero activity.

---

## 3. High/Low Season Forecasting

**Question:** _"What is the general trend of high vs. low seasons?"_

### Talking Points:

- **Price Elasticity**: The system tracks how prices "breathe" in response to demand. In high seasons, we observe the **"Strike-through Price"** disappearing as hotels remove discounts.
- **Market Velocity**: In high season (e.g., School Holidays), the "Inventory Depletion Rate" is high—rooms disappear minutes after being listed. In low season, inventory remains static for days.
- **The "Heat Map"**: By looking at the **Analytics & Trends** tab, we can visualize months where the total "State Collection" is at risk versus months where supply is naturally low.

---

## 4. The Power of Government Integration (The "Missing Link")

**Question:** _"Why do we need the Official Government Data (Registration & Tax Submissions)?"_

### Talking Points:

- **Data Integrity**: OTA data (Agoda/Booking) is "Market Signal," but **Government Data** is "Legal Reality." Only the Government has the official record of who _should_ be paying and what they _claim_ to have sold.
- **The Two Pillars**:
  1.  **Property Registration (`property_registration.csv`)**: Tells us which hotels are legally licensed. Without this, we cannot identify "Illegal/Ghost Properties" that are operating under the radar.
  2.  **Submitted Records (`submitted_records.csv`)**: The actual numbers the hotel reported for tax. This is the baseline for honesty.

---

## 5. The Audit Matrix: Scraped vs. Actual vs. Expected

**Question:** _"How do we prove a hotel is under-reporting?"_

### Talking Points:

- **The Three-Way Join**: Our system performs a unique "Data Fusion":
  - **SCRAPED DATA (The Truth)**: What the public sees. If Agoda says a hotel sold 100 nights, those 100 nights happened.
  - **SUBMITTED DATA (The Claim)**: What the hotel tells the state.
  - **EXPECTED TAX (The Target)**: The math doesn't lie. `(Scraped Nights - Submitted Nights) * Tax Rate = Tax Leakage`.
- **Gap Identification**: By joining these datasets, the dashboard highlights specific properties where the **Scraped Nights > Submitted Nights**.
- **Actionable Enforcement**: We don't just say "tax is missing"; we provide the **exact property name, registration number, and the RM value** of the discrepancy.
- **Transparency Note (Active Supply vs. Total Rooms)**:
  - **Limitation**: OTAs (Agoda/Booking) do not reveal a hotel's _total_ room count (e.g., they won't say "this hotel has 50 rooms in total").
  - **Solution**: Our system uses **"Active Supply"** (rooms currently listed for sale) as a smart proxy. This means our tax leakage estimates are **conservative**—the real discrepancy is likely even higher if the hotel has "unlisted" rooms being sold via walk-ins.

---

## 6. Why This Matters for the State

- **Identifying Leakage**: If a hotel is "Busy" on Agoda/Booking but "Quiet" in tax submissions, that is **Tax Leakage**.
- **Fair Competition**: Ensures registered hotels paying their dues aren't being undercut by "ghost" properties (unregistered Airbnbs) that the system also tracks.
- **Batch Intelligence**: This system provides a comprehensive audit of the Pahang tourism economy based on historical batch data, allowing for deeper analysis of past performance.

---

## 7. Fuzzy Intelligence: Matching the Data

**Question:** _"How do we link a name on Agoda to a name in the Government records if they are spelled differently?"_

### Talking Points:

- **The Matching Challenge**: A hotel might be listed as _"The Grand Ion"_ on Agoda but registered as _"Ion Delemen Hotel"_ in government records.
- **Fuzzy Substring Logic**: The system uses intelligent "Fuzzy Matching." It strips away common words, normalizes the text, and looks for overlapping patterns.
- **Zero Manual Work**: This eliminates the need for staff to manually match thousands of rows of data, making the audit process instantaneous.

---

---

## Technical Edge:

- **Multi-Platform**: Cross-references Agoda and Booking.com to ensure we don't miss "hidden" inventory.
- **Anti-Bot Stealth**: The system mimics human behavior to ensure data collection is never interrupted by platform blocks.
- **Weekly Batch Pipeline**: With the `--week` flag, the system performs a "Weekly Job"—processing an entire week of historical data in one batch to identify long-term discrepancies.
- **Manual Control**: The system is designed to be triggered **manually**, giving the state full control over when audits are performed and ensuring data integrity before each run.

---

## 8. Known Bottlenecks (The Reality Check)

- **Not Real-Time**: This is the primary bottleneck. The system does **not** catch every single room change the second it happens. It works in **Batch Mode**, processing data from specific time periods.
- **Manual Execution Needed**: Because it is batch-based, it requires a staff member to manually start the process to get the latest analysis.
- **Offline Gaps**: Direct walk-ins that aren't listed on Agoda/Booking cannot be tracked digitally. Our data is a "Top-Tier Proxy," not a total replacement for physical checks.

## Appendix: How the Risk Engine Works

The system automatically categorizes every property into one of four risk levels based on the join between **OTA Data** and **Government Records**:

| Flag            | Logic / Condition                             | Business Meaning                                                                        |
| :-------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **🔴 CRITICAL** | **Not Registered** + **Scraped Activity > 0** | **Illegal Operation**: Property is selling online but not in the registration database. |
| **🟠 HIGH**     | **Registered** + **Missing Nights > 100**     | **Severe Under-reporting**: Significant gap between public sales and declarations.      |
| **🟡 WARN**     | **Registered** + **Missing Nights > 0**       | **Minor Discrepancy**: Small gaps that require monitoring.                              |
| **🟢 OK**       | **Registered** + **Missing Nights ≤ 0**       | **Compliant**: Reported data matches or exceeds detected OTA activity.                  |
